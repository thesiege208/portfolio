Hello there!

My name is Sijing Xie and I've been a technical writer since 2019.

Here you'll find examples and explanations of my work, which includes technical documentation, instructional videos, and web development.

How it all began:

During a game design course at UC Berkeley, my friend, who had no prior experience with Git, asked for a tutorial on the basic Git commands so that she could access and use our project repository. At her request, I wrote this short and informal guide for her, providing just enough context so she would understand why certain actions were required.

How it's going:

In 2019, I started at Veeva Systems, Inc. as a technical writing intern and was soon promoted to Associate Technical Writer on the CRM documentation team. Two years later, I was promoted again to Technical Writer.

As part of the CRM documentation team, my responsibilities include:

  • writing admin-focused release notes for my assigned areas of the Veeva CRM and Vault CRM applications
  • responding to feedback emails from readers
  • maintaining our developer documentation
  • creating end user-focused instructional videos to accompany written documentation, including script writing and video editing
  • creating admin-focused Arcade demos to accompany written documentation
  • writing and managing release notes for patches and hotfixes
  • writing end user-focused training courses for CRM Flightpath
  • writing internal admin documentation for internal tools and software

I became proficient with the various tools and software we commonly use, including:

  • MadCap Flare (completed MadCap Advanced Developer (MAD) Certification in 2020)
  • GitLab
  • Confluence
  • Jira
  • Camtasia
  • WellSaid
  • Arcade Software
  • Salesforce (became a Salesforce Certified Administrator (SCA) in 2022)

Additionally, I played a more technical role on the team thanks to my previous coding experience and provided support in maintaining and designing our websites. In 2022, when we started Flightpath to host end user-focused training material, I took charge of developing the homepage and the feedback/review modal using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
